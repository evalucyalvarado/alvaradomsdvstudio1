The Black Panthers: Social Networks in The Smithsonian Archives

The Black Panther Party, founded in 1966 by Huey P. Newton and Bobby Seale in Oakland, California, emerged as a groundbreaking force in the struggle for Black Liberation. Born out of criticisms of Non-Voilent tactics used in the Civil Rights Movement, the Panthers sought to empower their communities through self-defense, education, and social programs. The foundational principles of solidarity and mutual support within the organization is vividly illustrated through the photographs in this collection, which capture moments of support and unity between leadership and ordinary members alike. The "unknown" subjects featured in these images are situated in the context of the known network within the party. By providing connecting these unknown people to major members of the party, this project offers an opportunity for further discovery and connection with living members of the party and their decendents.
